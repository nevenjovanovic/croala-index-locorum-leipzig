Conclusion

We have set out to annotate place names in selected Croatian Latin texts from the Renaissance.
These texts use named entities from the antiquity to denote places in later periods as well.

Our comments qualify the placeness of place names, describe their linguistic form, link the words to things (real or imaginary places), and locate the things in time (which is culturally determined, and which be real or imaginary too).

We were able to put it all together by connecting annotations to text segments via URNs.
We used the CITE Architecture as a blueprint for our infrastructure.
